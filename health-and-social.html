Work Commutes

Since the urban sprawl is focused around D.C., a large portion of the people living in these areas have jobs in D.C. which require very long commutes. D.C.-area workers have among the longest commutes in the country -- an average of 47 minutes, compared to the national average of 27 minutes (Source).

And the length of commute matters a lot to happiness of commuters.

Of course, people don't deliberately seek out longer commutes -- they're forced into them by the fact that all available housing (at prices they can afford) is far away from the places that most people work. (But we'll talk more about that in the final section.)
